Desperate Housewives is an American mystery comedy-drama television series created by Marc Cherry, and produced by ABC Studios and Cherry Productions. It aired for eight seasons on ABC from October 3, 2004, until May 13, 2012, for a total of 180 episodes. Executive producer Marc Cherry served as showrunner. Other executive producers since the fourth season included Bob Daily, George W. Perkins, John Pardee, Joey Murphy, David Grossman, and Larry Shaw. Set on Wisteria Lane, a street in the fictional town of Fairview in the fictional Eagle State, Desperate Housewives follows the lives of a group of women as seen through the eyes of their friend and neighbor who commits suicide in the pilot episode. The series covers fifteen years of the women's lives, which includes a five-year timeline jump in the series' narrative. The women work through domestic struggles and family life, while being faced with the secrets, crimes, and mysteries hidden behind the doors of their on-the-surface—beautiful, and seemingly perfect, suburban neighborhood.

Main Characters

  1. Susan Mayer (played by Teri Hatcher)

    • Personality: Susan is the lovable, quirky, and often clumsy protagonist of the show. She is a single mother who is deeply romantic, somewhat naive, and always in pursuit of true love. Despite her awkwardness and sometimes over-the-top behavior, Susan has a heart of gold and is genuinely caring for those around her.
    • Story Arc: Susan’s main storyline revolves around her relationships, particularly with Mike Delfino, whom she has a turbulent yet passionate romance with. She also faces struggles as a single mother to her daughter, Julie, and navigates various personal and family issues.

  2. Bree Van de Kamp (played by Marcia Cross)

    • Personality: Bree is the epitome of perfectionism. A devoutly religious, highly organized, and meticulous housewife, she is obsessed with maintaining an idealized version of suburban life. Bree has an intense need for control, both in her home and personal relationships, but her seemingly perfect exterior often hides deep insecurities and personal struggles.
    • Story Arc: Throughout the series, Bree’s life unravels as she deals with her troubled marriage to Rex, the challenges of raising her children, and her own issues with perfectionism. She is also involved in several complicated romantic relationships, including a marriage to Orson Hodge. Bree's character evolves into a strong, independent woman who learns to embrace imperfection.

  3. Lynette Scavo (played by Felicity Huffman)

    • Personality: Lynette is the overworked and often frazzled mother of four young children. She is a practical and down-to-earth woman, always trying to juggle her family, career, and marriage. Lynette’s struggles are largely centered around motherhood and maintaining a sense of balance in her chaotic life.
    • Story Arc: Lynette faces numerous challenges, including dealing with her husband's past infidelity, the stresses of raising four children, and her career aspirations. She often feels overwhelmed by the demands of her life but is determined to make it work. Her growth as a mother and wife is one of the central themes of her character.

  4. Gabrielle Solis (played by Eva Longoria)

    • Personality: Gabrielle is a former model turned stay-at-home housewife. Initially, she comes across as a glamorous, materialistic, and somewhat self-centered character. However, over time, Gabrielle's character reveals a deeper side, and she evolves into someone who values family and love more than just material wealth.
    • Story Arc: Gabrielle’s journey revolves around her marriage to Carlos, their struggles with fertility, and later, their adoption of a child. Gabrielle also has to reconcile her past, including her infidelity and selfish behaviors, with her desire to be a better person and mother. Her character undergoes significant growth as she learns the importance of selflessness and love.

  5. Edie Britt (played by Nicollette Sheridan)

    • Personality: Edie is the seductive and manipulative woman of Wisteria Lane. Initially introduced as a seemingly confident and promiscuous woman, Edie is often the antagonist of the series. She is determined to get what she wants, whether it's a man or revenge, and isn’t afraid to use her charm and sexuality to manipulate situations.
    • Story Arc: Edie’s character is marked by her turbulent relationships with the other women, especially with Susan, as she competes for Mike's affection. Throughout the series, Edie is involved in several dramatic situations, including her failed relationships and attempts at starting anew. Her character is eventually written out, but her impact on the show is significant due to her dramatic influence on the dynamics of the neighborhood.
